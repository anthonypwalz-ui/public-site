Electrical Safety Checklist for Homeowners

You don’t need to be an electrician to do a basic safety walkthrough. Here’s a practical checklist to identify hazards before they become emergencies.

A Practical Checklist to Protect Your Home

The items below can be checked by any homeowner with no special tools. If you find anything concerning, note it and call a licensed electrician. Many of these issues are inexpensive to fix early and become much more expensive — or dangerous — if left alone.

Outlets and Switches

  • All outlets in kitchens, bathrooms, garages, basements, and outdoors are GFCI-protected (press TEST — the outlet should lose power; press RESET to restore)
  • No outlets are warm or hot to the touch
  • No discoloration, burn marks, or cracked cover plates anywhere in the home
  • Plugs fit snugly in all outlets — none slide out on their own
  • No two-prong (ungrounded) outlets in bedrooms, home offices, or kitchens
  • No outlets visibly overloaded with power strips or adapter stacks

Panel and Breakers

  • Panel door closes and latches properly
  • No rust, corrosion, or moisture inside or around the panel
  • All breakers are labeled and in the ON position (except any intentionally turned off)
  • No double-tapped breakers (two wires sharing one breaker terminal, unless rated for it)
  • Panel is not buzzing, humming, or crackling
  • Panel door does not feel warm to the touch

Cords and Wiring

  • No extension cords being used as permanent wiring
  • No cords running under rugs or through walls
  • No cords with cracked, frayed, or taped insulation
  • No appliance cords pinched by furniture or bent sharply at the plug
  • No power strips daisy-chained (strip plugged into strip)

Smoke and CO Detectors

  • Smoke detectors present on every floor and inside every bedroom
  • All detectors tested within the last 6 months
  • All detectors are less than 10 years old (sensor lifespan)
  • CO detector present on every floor and near sleeping areas

Exterior and Outdoor

  • All exterior outlets have weatherproof in-use covers
  • No exposed wiring on the exterior of the home
  • Overhead service entrance wires are intact and not touching tree branches
  • All outdoor lights and outlets are GFCI-protected

When to Call an Electrician

If you checked any of the above and found a concern — warm outlet, loose breaker, burn marks, double-tapped breaker, or ungrounded outlets throughout the home — get it on a professional’s schedule. Many of these issues are straightforward repairs. All of them become more serious and expensive if ignored.
