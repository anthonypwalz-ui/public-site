Signs You Need an Electrician Immediately

Most home maintenance can wait for a convenient appointment. Some electrical problems cannot. Here’s how to know the difference.

Some Electrical Problems Can’t Wait

The difference between an urgent electrical issue and an emergency one is often whether a fire can start before a technician arrives. Know which signs mean act now, and which mean schedule this week.

Call Immediately for These Signs

Burning smell from an outlet, switch, or panel. Something is overheating right now. Turn off the breaker if you can do so safely, stop using the area, and call. Do not assume the smell is temporary.

Sparks from an outlet or panel. A spark you can hear and see is arcing. Large or repeated sparks from the same location indicate serious faults that can start fires inside walls.

Buzzing, humming, or crackling from the panel. Panels should be nearly silent. Electrical sounds from a panel indicate loose connections or arcing inside the enclosure — a potential fire risk.

Outlets that are hot to the touch. Outlets should be room temperature. Heat means resistance inside your wall right now.

Smoke or scorch marks around any electrical device. Something has already overheated. Do not continue using it.

Lights flickering across the entire house simultaneously. Whole-house flicker points to a problem at the service entrance or main panel — a utility or electrician emergency.

Wet electrical panel or outlets. Water and electricity are a deadly combination. If an electrical component is wet, do not touch it — turn off the main breaker from a dry location and call immediately.

Breaker that won’t stay on and serves critical equipment. If a breaker controlling heating, medical equipment, or refrigeration won’t stay on, address it immediately.

Emergency vs. Urgent

Emergency (stop using affected electrical areas now): burning smell, smoke, sparks from panel or outlet, wet electrical components, scorch marks with no clear explanation.

Urgent (schedule within 24–48 hours): repeatedly tripping breakers with no clear cause, warm outlets, lights flickering throughout the house, one “leg” of power down.

What to Do While Waiting

Turn off the relevant breaker. Unplug devices from affected outlets. Keep the area clear. If you smell smoke or see flames, evacuate and call 911 before calling an electrician. Your safety always comes first — the wiring can be repaired, but it can’t help you if you’re in danger.
