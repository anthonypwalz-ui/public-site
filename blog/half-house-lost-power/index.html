Half of My House Lost Power

When only one side of your home goes dark, it’s a split-phase electrical issue. Here’s what’s happening and why you should act quickly.

Why Half a House?

American homes use a 240V split-phase electrical service. Power arrives from the utility on two “legs” (L1 and L2), each carrying 120V. Half your breakers run off L1 and half off L2. When you lose power to exactly half the house — but not all of it — one of those legs has failed or is partially interrupted.

Common Causes

Utility-side failure. One of the utility’s supply conductors to your home can fail or disconnect. If you lose power to exactly half the house and your neighbors are unaffected, this is likely the cause. Call your utility company first before calling an electrician.

Tripped double-pole breaker. Large appliances like dryers, air conditioners, and water heaters use double-pole breakers that connect to both legs. If a double-pole breaker trips, it can shut down a significant portion of the house depending on how circuits are arranged in the panel.

Loose service entrance connection. The connection between the utility lines and your main panel can corrode or loosen over time. A partial connection means one leg delivers reduced voltage or none at all.

Failed main breaker. Main breakers can fail in a way that only partially trips — interrupting one leg while the other continues. The result looks exactly like a split-phase utility failure.

Is This an Emergency?

Yes. A partial loss of service can cause overvoltage on the remaining leg. Appliances, smart devices, and electronics running on the unaffected circuits may receive more than 120V, which can damage or destroy them. Unplug expensive electronics and call an electrician or your utility company immediately.

What Not to Do

Do not attempt to reset the main breaker repeatedly. Do not open the main panel or the utility meter enclosure — the meter enclosure contains utility-owned, always-energized equipment regardless of breaker position. If the panel is humming, buzzing, or sparking, do not open it at all. Call immediately.
